What are Internships/Practices for Students in Panama?

Real Experience

An internship/professional practice is a supervised work experience in a company or organization, where you apply the knowledge acquired academically in a real environment.

Part of the Study Plan

In many cases it is considered part of the study plan for graduation, fulfilling academic requirements while gaining practical experience.

Learning by Doing Program

In Panama there is the "Aprender Haciendo" (Learning by Doing) program from MITRADEL that seeks to place young people aged 17 to 24 in private companies through internships.

âœ… Common Requirements for Students

Be Enrolled
Be enrolled in an educational institution (university or technical) or be close to graduating. For example, the Technological University of Panama requires having completed at least half of the study plan to apply for a professional practice.
Documentation
Submit resume, copy of ID card (or identification) and, in some cases, student or accident insurance policy.
University Letter
Letter from the university (or authorization from the institution) indicating that the practice is part of the study plan or is an academic requirement.
Schedule Availability
Have schedule availability that is compatible with your classes; many practices allow "part-time" or adapt to the academic schedule.
Academic Performance
In some cases, good academic performance, English proficiency or other competencies according to the company. For certain programs, a good academic index is required.

Real Company Experience

You will learn from professional practice, work on projects that may be relevant to your career.

Skills Development

Teamwork, communication, responsibility, problem-solving ability and applying theory to practice.

Employment Opportunity

Many companies consider interns for jobs after successfully completing their internship.

Certification or Practice Letter

Many institutions provide proof that you completed the practice, which is good support for your CV.

Regulation and Incentives

The "Aprender Haciendo" (Learning by Doing) program stipulates a monthly allowance for the intern (B/. 450) and risk insurance for young people aged 18 to 25.

Duration and Modality

Typical duration: 6 months or equivalent to 130 working days. Modality: in-person, mixed or flexible according to the company.

ðŸ“Œ How to Apply (Typical Steps)

1. Verify Requirements

Verify that you meet the requirements: being enrolled/university, study levels, competencies, schedule availability.

2. Prepare Documentation

Prepare your resume (CV), cover letter and in some cases a letter from your university authorizing the practice.

3. Send Documentation

Send the documentation as indicated by the company or organization (email, online form, etc.).

4. Interview or Evaluation

Interview or technical evaluation (according to company) and selection.

5. Agreement Signing

Signing of agreement or practice agreement (if applicable), duration, supervision, schedule, responsibilities.

6. Start and Follow-up

Start of practice, follow-up, evaluation and finally receive proof or completion letter.

Can I do it while studying?

Yes, many practices are designed for active students and allow flexible schedules that adapt to your classes.

How many hours per week?

It varies according to the company and program. Many are part-time and adapt to the student's academic schedule.

Is it paid?

It is not always paid. However, the "Aprender Haciendo" (Learning by Doing) program does stipulate a monthly allowance of B/. 450 for young people aged 18-25.

How long does an internship last?

It can vary. Professional practice regulations indicate, for example, 6 months or equivalent to "130 working days" to complete.

Can I do it outside Panama City?

It depends on the company or organization. Some offer remote modalities or have presence in other provinces.

What modalities are available?

Many are in-person, but there are companies that allow mixed or flexible modality (especially for students) as long as it doesn't conflict with your classes.
